About the Researchers

Who builds each SignSight component

Four focused research tracks powering dynamic recognition, speech-to-sign conversion, interactive learning, and emotionally aware child support.

About the Researcher

Mark J J - focused on accessible AI for dynamic sign recognition

Mark J J is a final-year BSc (Hons) in Information Technology student at SLIIT, with a focus on applied machine learning for social impact. For SignSight, he designed the dynamic sign language recognition pipeline - webcam-based gesture capture, landmark feature engineering, Bi-LSTM / Bi-GRU modelling, and real-time inference using MediaPipe and OpenCV.

He is interested in making computer vision cheaper, clearer, and more useful outside the lab for underserved communities. This research project is his contribution toward that goal.

About the Researcher

Farsith M F M - focused on Tamil speech and sign bridging

Farsith M F M is a final-year BSc (Hons) in Information Technology student at SLIIT, with a focus on speech recognition and sign language translation. For SignSight, he designed the full audio and video to TSL conversion pipeline - speech-to-text processing, text alignment, LSTM-based sign mapping, and real-time output delivery using Flask and React.

He is interested in breaking down communication barriers for Tamil-speaking hearing-impaired communities through accessible and affordable language technology. This research project is his contribution toward that goal.

About the Researcher

Nethmi G K - focused on interactive sign language education

Nethmi G K is a final-year BSc (Hons) in Information Technology student at SLIIT, with a focus on educational technology and explainable machine learning. For SignSight, she designed the TSL Learning and Evaluation Platform - interactive lesson modules, multi-level quiz systems, video-based sign assessment using MediaPipe and RandomForest, and SHAP-driven performance feedback for both students and mentors.

She is interested in making sign language education structured, measurable, and accessible for Tamil-speaking learners who have historically lacked dedicated learning tools. This research project is her contribution toward that goal.

About the Researcher

Sandamini G H D H - focused on emotionally aware assistive technology

Sandamini G H D H is a final-year BSc (Hons) in Information Technology student at SLIIT, with a focus on affective computing and child-centred assistive systems. For SignSight, she designed the emotion-aware TSL support module for children - facial emotion detection using MobileNetV2 and MediaPipe, confidence and consistency analysis, and automated HTML report generation delivered to guardians via SMTP email.

She is interested in making assistive technology emotionally intelligent and accessible for hearing-impaired children, ensuring caregivers stay informed and engaged in the learning process. This research project is her contribution toward that goal.
